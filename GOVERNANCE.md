# Governance

ReproProof begins as a single-maintainer project. The maintainer owns releases, security response, roadmap decisions, and repository administration while accepting evidence-based community input.

## Decision process

Small reversible decisions are made in reviewed pull requests. Significant API, security, governance, or provider-policy changes require a public design issue and at least seven calendar days for feedback when practical. Security fixes may be handled privately and expedited.

## Roles

- Contributor: any person with an accepted contribution.
- Reviewer: a consistently constructive contributor trusted to review an area.
- Maintainer: a reviewer with merge/release responsibility and a demonstrated security and community track record.

No role is awarded to inflate contributor counts. Promotion is based on sustained, substantive work, judgment, availability, and conduct.

## Ownership and succession

The initial maintainer retains project ownership. After at least two trusted maintainers exist, critical repository and package permissions should require two people. If the initial maintainer is inactive for 90 days without notice, active maintainers may document and execute a succession plan through a public governance issue, subject to platform ownership rules.

## Conflicts of interest

Maintainers disclose relevant employer, vendor, or financial interests when deciding provider integrations or sponsorship. Sponsorship does not buy roadmap control or favorable benchmark treatment.
